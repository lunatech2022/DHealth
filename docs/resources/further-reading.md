# Further Reading

!!! info "Page status"
    **Level:** All levels &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Status:** :material-circle: Drafted &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-14

> **Purpose:** A curated, grouped reading list pointing to the primary literature behind the knowledge base, with a one-line description for each source.

## In one sentence

This is a starting bibliography — a short, grouped map of the key papers that underpin how we talk about the microbiome.

## Key points

- Sources are grouped by theme: **healthy microbiome & development**, **disease associations**, and **methods & analysis**.
- Each entry has a one-line description so you can choose what to read first.
- These are primary papers and major reviews; start with the reviews if you are new.
- Citations are exact — never alter an author, journal, year, or DOI when sharing them.

## The detail

The lists below are organised from foundational to specialised. If you are newer to the field, begin with the review articles in **Methods & analysis** and the **Human Microbiome Project**, then branch into the development and disease sections as questions come up. Every item links to its DOI via the footnote.

### Healthy microbiome & development

- A landmark map of the healthy human microbiome's structure, function, and diversity.[^hmp2012]
- How the gut microbiome differs across age and geography, underscoring that "normal" varies.[^yatsunenko2012]
- How microbial communities take shape and shift in the developing infant gut.[^koenig2011]
- Long-term tracking of how the gut microbiome develops through early childhood.[^stewart2018]

### Disease associations

- Gut microbiota signatures associated with early frailty.[^frailty2016]
- The gut microbiome studied in the context of early-onset type 1 diabetes.[^t1d2018]
- An association between gut *Akkermansia* and metabolic syndrome.[^akkermansia2021]

### Methods & analysis

- Best-practice guidance for analysing microbiomes end to end — a great orientation.[^knight2018]
- A practical review of how shotgun metagenomics works, from sampling to analysis.[^quince2017]
- How 16S rRNA gene sequencing performs for species- and strain-level analysis.[^johnson2019]
- Using meta-omics to push microbiome research toward function and translation.[^metaomics2019]
- A twin study using shotgun metagenomics to weigh genetic vs environmental influences on the gut.[^xie2016]

### Context reviews

- A clear overview of why a "healthy" microbiome changes across age, environment, diet, and disease.[^rinninella2019]

!!! tip "So what?"
    When a customer or colleague asks "where does this come from?", point them here. Leading with the primary literature builds trust and keeps claims grounded.

## Why it matters for Dayhoff / DHealth

A grounded, well-organised reading list is a credibility asset. Sales and new staff can use it to answer "what's the evidence?" with real sources rather than vague claims, and to deepen their own understanding at their own pace. Keeping citations exact — never inventing or altering a source — protects the integrity of every customer conversation.

## Common questions

- **"Where do I start?"** With the reviews in *Methods & analysis* and the Human Microbiome Project paper.
- **"Are these peer-reviewed?"** These are published primary papers and major reviews; each links to its DOI.
- **"Can I share these with a customer?"** Yes — just share them exactly as listed, without altering any detail.
