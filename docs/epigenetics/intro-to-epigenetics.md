# Introduction to Epigenetics

!!! info "Page status"
    **Level:** Beginner &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Status:** :material-circle: Drafted &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-14

> **Purpose:** Give every team member a clear, plain-language foundation for what epigenetics is and why gene expression can change without changing the underlying DNA.

## In one sentence

Epigenetics is the study of changes in how genes are switched on or off without any change to the DNA sequence itself.

## Key points

- Your DNA sequence is fixed, but which genes are active can change over time.
- These on/off settings are controlled by chemical "marks" layered on top of the DNA and the proteins that package it.
- The main mechanisms are DNA methylation, histone modification, nucleosome remodeling, and RNA-mediated targeting.[^dawson2012]
- Epigenetic marks respond to your environment, behavior, and life stage.[^moore2012]
- When these marks go wrong, they can contribute to disease, including cancer.[^dawson2012]

## The detail

Think of your DNA as the hardware of your body, a fixed set of instructions present in nearly every cell. Epigenetics is the software layer that decides which of those instructions actually get used in a given cell at a given time. A skin cell and a brain cell carry the same DNA, but they look and behave very differently because different genes are switched on and off. That switching is epigenetic.

Several mechanisms work together to control gene activity. The most studied is **DNA methylation**, the addition of a small chemical tag (a methyl group) onto the DNA at specific positions, which typically quiets a gene.[^moore2012] Other layers include **histone modification** (chemical changes to the proteins that DNA wraps around), **nucleosome remodeling** (repackaging DNA to expose or hide it), and **RNA-mediated targeting** (small RNA molecules that help direct where these marks land).[^dawson2012] Together these processes regulate the fundamental cellular programs that determine identity, growth, and function.

A defining feature of epigenetics is that it is **dynamic**. Marks are added and removed throughout development as cells specialize, and they can be modulated by environmental and lifestyle signals.[^moore2012] This is what makes epigenetics so interesting for health: it is a biological record of how genes have been used, and it can shift in response to what happens to us.

Because epigenetic marks control gene activity, errors in them carry real consequences. Misregulation of epigenetic processes can drive disease, and epigenetic changes are a well-established hallmark of cancer.[^dawson2012] Encouragingly, because these marks are chemical rather than fixed in the sequence, they are also potential targets for therapy, and a class of epigenetic drugs already exists.[^dawson2012]

!!! tip "So what?"
    Because epigenetic marks are dynamic and respond to environment and lifestyle, they offer a window into health that the DNA sequence alone cannot. They can change, which means they can be measured to track health and, in principle, influenced to improve it. That makes epigenetics a uniquely actionable layer of biology.

## Why it matters for Dayhoff / DHealth

Epigenetics is the scientific backbone of much of what DHealth measures. Understanding that gene activity (not just gene sequence) shapes health helps the team explain why our diagnostics look at methylation and other epigenetic signals rather than DNA sequence alone. This is the foundation customers need before any conversation about methylation testing or biological age makes sense.

## Common questions

**Is epigenetics the same as genetics?**
No. Genetics is about the DNA sequence you inherit. Epigenetics is about how that sequence is used, the on/off settings that can change without altering the sequence itself.

**Can epigenetic changes be reversed?**
Many are dynamic and can change over time, which is part of why they are so promising for both measurement and therapy.[^moore2012][^dawson2012]

**Why should a healthy person care about epigenetics?**
Because epigenetic marks shift with environment and lifestyle, they can reflect how the body is doing now, not just the fixed risks coded in DNA.
