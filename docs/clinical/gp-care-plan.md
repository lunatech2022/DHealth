# GP Care Plan: When & How to Use the Tests

!!! info "Page status"
    **Level:** Intermediate &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Status:** :material-circle: Drafted &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-14

> **Purpose:** A practical, recommended care-plan framework for GPs — what to test, when, how often, and how to explain results to patients, across microbiome, methylation, and the proteomic blood test.

!!! warning "Important — informational, not medical advice"
    This is a **suggested framework**, not a clinical protocol or standard of care. The tests are **informational** and are an **adjunct** to clinical judgment; they do not diagnose, treat, or cure disease. Individualise everything to the patient, and route diagnosis, prescriptions, and any medication dosing (including GLP-1) to the appropriate clinician.

## In one sentence

Match the **test to the concern**, set a **baseline**, intervene mostly with lifestyle, **re-measure** on a sensible cadence, and explain results in plain, modifiable terms.[^fan2020][^moore2012]

## Key points

- The microbiome and methylation are **modifiable**, so they suit a *measure → intervene → re-measure* loop; the blood test adds a real-time systemic snapshot.
- Choose tests by **indication** (see table), not reflexively.
- Re-test cadence is driven by how fast each marker changes: microbiome in **weeks–months**, methylation **slower (6–12 months)**.
- Explaining results well — plain language, modifiable framing, one clear next step — is most of the value.

!!! abstract "In simple terms"
    Pick the test that fits why the patient came in, take a "before" picture, help them change diet/sleep/stress, then take an "after" picture a few months later to see if it worked. Explain it like a coach, not a lab printout.

## When to consider testing (indications)

Common reasons a GP might reach for these tests, and which test fits:

| Presenting concern / indication | Microbiome | Proteomic blood | Methylation | What it adds |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weight, metabolic risk, or on/considering a GLP-1 | ✅ | ✅ | ◻ optional | Functional + systemic picture of metabolic status; track over time[^fan2020][^glp1micro2023] |
| Persistent gut symptoms (bloating, IBD-type, irregularity) | ✅ | ◻ | ◻ | Strain-level taxa + inflammatory vs SCFA-producing capacity[^quince2017][^henke2019][^uc2021] |
| Low energy / fatigue / general wellness | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | Diversity, functional output, and biological-age context[^frailty2016][^microbiomeage2025] |
| Mood / stress / cognitive concerns | ✅ | ◻ | ◻ | Gut–brain axis and microbial-metabolite context[^sorboni2022] |
| Healthy aging / longevity focus | ◻ | ◻ | ✅ | Biological age and pace-of-aging tracking[^moore2012][^microbiomeage2025] |
| Athletic recovery / body composition | ✅ | ✅ | ◻ optional | Recovery, barrier function, nutritional/metabolic markers (see [For Practitioners](for-practitioners.md)) |

✅ = primary fit &nbsp;·&nbsp; ◻ = optional / situational. Map indications to the [Four Pillars](four-pillars.md) where helpful.

## How to use them (the loop)

1. **Baseline.** Order the test(s) that match the concern. Often the microbiome alone; add blood and/or methylation where the indication warrants.
2. **Interpret in context.** Read alongside history, exam, and existing labs — never in isolation. Flag anything needing diagnosis or referral.
3. **Intervene (lifestyle first).** Targeted diet and fibre, sleep, stress, and nutrition are the levers that move the microbiome and methylation.[^fan2020][^moore2012][^scfa2023]
4. **Re-measure.** Re-test on the cadence below to see whether markers moved; adjust.
5. **Coordinate care.** Send anything medical — diagnosis, prescriptions, dosing — to the appropriate clinician.

## How often (re-test cadence)

General guidance — individualise to the patient and the intervention:

- **Microbiome:** baseline, then **~every 3 months** during an active intervention (it shifts over weeks to months); stretch to 6–12 months for maintenance.
- **Proteomic blood:** **every 2–3 months** when actively tracking a metabolic or recovery goal; otherwise periodically.
- **Methylation / biological age:** **every 6–12 months** — it changes slowly and integrates cumulative lifestyle, so frequent testing adds little.[^moore2012]

!!! tip "So what?"
    The cadence is the point: a single result is a snapshot, but a baseline-and-recheck story is what shows the patient (and you) that the plan is working.

## How to explain outcomes to patients

Keep it plain, modifiable, and actionable. Useful scripts:

- **Framing the whole thing:** "This is a detailed picture of your gut ecosystem and how your body is running right now. It's not a diagnosis — it's a map we can act on and re-check."
- **A balanced / healthy result:** "Your community looks diverse and is producing the helpful compounds we want. Let's protect that with [specific habits] and re-check in a few months."
- **A result to work on:** "We're seeing lower diversity and fewer of the fibre-fermenting microbes that calm inflammation. The good news is this is **modifiable** — here's the one change I'd start with."
- **Always end with one next step.** One clear action beats ten. Then book the re-check.
- **Stay in lane:** anything that looks medical ("this could indicate X disease") goes to proper evaluation, not a microbiome read.

## Why it matters for Dayhoff / DHealth

A clean, honest care-plan framework lowers the barrier for GPs to adopt the tests: it tells them exactly what to order, when, how often, and what to say. That repeatability — and the re-check loop — is what turns a one-off test into ongoing, defensible value.

## Common questions

**"Do I always need all three tests?"**
No. Start with the test that matches the concern; add others only when they answer a real question.

**"How soon will a re-test show change?"**
Microbiome can shift within weeks to a few months on a real intervention; methylation moves slowly. Match cadence to the marker.[^moore2012]

**"What do I tell a patient with a 'bad' result?"**
That it is informative, not a diagnosis, and — importantly — **modifiable**, then give one concrete next step and a re-check date.[^fan2020]
