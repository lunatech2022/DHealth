# Applications & Use Cases

!!! info "Page status"
    **Level:** Beginner &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Status:** :material-circle: Drafted &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-14

> **Purpose:** Give the team a plain-language map of where microbiome (and methylation / biological-age) testing is used today, so you can speak confidently about real-world value.

## In one sentence

Microbiome and related molecular tests turn a sample into a readout of which microbes are present and what biological signals the body is sending, with uses spanning everyday wellness, research into disease, and measures of aging.

## Key points

- The gut microbiome influences digestion, metabolism, immunity, and even brain signalling, which is why testing has so many applications.
- Use cases fall into three buckets: **wellness and optimisation**, **disease-association research**, and **aging / biological-age** measures.
- Microbiome shifts have been linked to metabolic conditions and to neurological health through the gut-brain axis.
- Methylation-based tests look at chemical marks on DNA and are used to estimate biological age, a complement to microbiome work.
- Most consumer-facing uses today are about insight and optimisation, not diagnosis.

## The detail

**Wellness and optimisation.** The most common everyday use is helping people understand their gut. A healthy gut microbiome is diverse and balanced, and its composition is shaped by diet, environment, and lifestyle.[^rinninella2019] Testing gives individuals a baseline they can track over time as they adjust food, fibre, and habits.

**Disease-association research.** The microbiome sits at the centre of human metabolic health and disease, with strong links to how we process energy and store fat.[^fan2020] Research has tied specific microbial patterns to conditions: longitudinal work followed the gut microbiome in children developing early-onset type 1 diabetes,[^t1d2018] and the bacterium *Akkermansia* has been associated with metabolic syndrome.[^akkermansia2021] In inflammatory bowel conditions, network analyses have identified specific microbial species relevant to ulcerative colitis.[^uc2021] These are associations that power research and risk stratification, not standalone diagnoses.

**The gut-brain axis.** A growing body of evidence connects gut microbes to neurological health. A comprehensive review documented the microbiome's role across a range of human neurological disorders, reinforcing why "gut health" and "brain health" are increasingly discussed together.[^sorboni2022]

**Aging and biological age.** Two complementary approaches estimate how the body is aging. Microbiome composition itself carries an age signal: models can estimate chronological age from human microbiomes.[^microbiomeage2025] Separately, **DNA methylation** (chemical marks added to DNA that switch genes on or off) is a foundational biological mechanism,[^moore2012] and methylation patterns underpin many biological-age "clocks." Together these give a richer picture of aging than birthdays alone.

!!! tip "So what?"
    When a customer asks "what can this actually do for me?", anchor on the three buckets. Most people start with wellness and optimisation; the disease-association and aging science is what makes the field credible and exciting, even when individual tests are not diagnostic.

## Why it matters for Dayhoff / DHealth

These applications are the story behind every sales conversation. Understanding that microbiome testing spans wellness, research, and aging lets you meet customers where they are, set honest expectations (insight and tracking, not diagnosis), and connect the science to outcomes people care about: energy, gut comfort, metabolic health, and healthy aging.

## Common questions

- **Is this a medical diagnosis?** No. Most microbiome testing provides insight and a baseline to track, not a clinical diagnosis.
- **Can it tell me about disease risk?** The research links microbial patterns to conditions, but those are associations studied in populations, not personal verdicts.
- **What is biological age?** An estimate of how your body is aging based on molecular signals such as DNA methylation, which can differ from your calendar age.
